# MFRS Framework Reference (Malaysian Financial Reporting Standards)

Applies to: **Berhad (public-listed companies)** and entities that voluntarily adopt full MFRS.

MFRS is word-for-word equivalent to IFRS as issued by the IASB, with effective dates aligned by MASB.

---

## Key Standards and Their Application

### MFRS 1 — First-time Adoption of MFRS
- Transitioning entities: apply MFRS retrospectively at transition date
- Prepare opening MFRS statement of financial position
- Limited exemptions available (e.g., deemed cost for PPE, cumulative translation differences)

### MFRS 2 — Share-based Payment
- Equity-settled: measure at grant-date fair value, expense over vesting period
- Cash-settled: measure at each reporting date fair value, expense over vesting period
- Most common in listed entities: ESOS, share grants to directors

### MFRS 7 — Financial Instruments: Disclosures
- Significance of financial instruments to financial position and performance
- Nature and extent of risks (credit risk, liquidity risk, market risk)
- Sensitivity analysis required for market risks
- More extensive than MPERS S11

### MFRS 9 — Financial Instruments (replaces MFRS 139)
**Classification:**
- Amortised cost: business model = hold to collect, SPPI test passed
- FVOCI: business model = hold to collect AND sell, SPPI test passed
- FVTPL: all others, or designated irrevocably

**Impairment (Expected Credit Loss model):**
- Stage 1: 12-month ECL (performing)
- Stage 2: Lifetime ECL (significant increase in credit risk)
- Stage 3: Lifetime ECL (credit-impaired)
- Simplified approach available for trade receivables (always lifetime ECL)

**Hedge accounting:**
- Cash flow hedges, fair value hedges, net investment hedges
- Effectiveness testing: economic relationship, credit risk doesn't dominate, hedge ratio appropriate

### MFRS 10 — Consolidated Financial Statements
- Control = power + exposure to variable returns + ability to use power
- Consolidate all subsidiaries (no size exemption under MFRS)
- Eliminate intragroup transactions
- NCI measured at fair value or proportionate share (policy choice per acquisition)

### MFRS 12 — Disclosure of Interests in Other Entities
- Subsidiaries, joint arrangements, associates, unconsolidated structured entities
- Significant judgements and assumptions
- Nature of risks associated with interests

### MFRS 13 — Fair Value Measurement
- Fair value hierarchy: Level 1 (quoted prices), Level 2 (observable inputs), Level 3 (unobservable)
- Exit price in principal market
- Applies when another MFRS requires/permits FV measurement

### MFRS 15 — Revenue from Contracts with Customers
**5-Step Model:**
1. Identify the contract(s)
2. Identify performance obligations (PO)
3. Determine transaction price (variable consideration, significant financing, non-cash, payable to customer)
4. Allocate transaction price to POs (standalone selling price)
5. Recognise revenue when/as PO is satisfied (point in time vs over time)

**Over time criteria** (any one):
- Customer simultaneously receives and consumes benefits
- Asset with no alternative use + enforceable right to payment for performance to date
- Entity's performance creates/enhances asset customer controls

**Common applications:**
- Professional services: over time (customer consumes simultaneously)
- Equipment sales: point in time (transfer of control)
- Construction contracts: over time (asset on customer's land)
- Software licences: right to use (point in time) vs right to access (over time)

### MFRS 16 — Leases
**Lessee accounting:**
- Recognise right-of-use (ROU) asset + lease liability for ALL leases (no finance/operating distinction)
- Exceptions: short-term leases (< 12 months), low-value assets (< ~USD5,000)
- Initial measurement: PV of lease payments using implicit rate or incremental borrowing rate
- Subsequent: ROU depreciated over shorter of lease term or useful life; liability accrete interest + reduce by payments
- Reassess on modification, change in lease term, change in payments

**Lessor accounting:**
- Classify as finance lease or operating lease (same as IAS 17)
- Finance lease: derecognise asset, recognise receivable
- Operating lease: retain asset, recognise income straight-line

### MFRS 101 — Presentation of Financial Statements
**Complete set:**
1. Statement of Financial Position (as at period end + comparative)
2. Statement of Profit or Loss and Other Comprehensive Income
3. Statement of Changes in Equity
4. Statement of Cash Flows
5. Notes (significant accounting policies + other explanatory)
6. Statement of Financial Position at beginning of earliest comparative (if retrospective restatement)

**Current/non-current classification:**
- Current: expected to be realised/settled within 12 months or normal operating cycle
- Operating cycle: if not clearly identifiable, assume 12 months

### MFRS 102 — Inventories (= IAS 2)
- Cost: purchase + conversion + other costs to bring to present location/condition
- Cost formulas: FIFO or weighted average (LIFO prohibited)
- Measurement: lower of cost and NRV
- NRV: estimated selling price less estimated costs to complete and sell

### MFRS 108 — Accounting Policies, Changes in Estimates and Errors
- Changes in policy: retrospective (restate comparatives)
- Changes in estimate: prospective
- Prior period errors: retrospective restatement

### MFRS 110 — Events After the Reporting Period
- Adjusting events: conditions existed at reporting date → adjust
- Non-adjusting events: arose after → disclose if material (e.g., major acquisition, natural disaster)
- Dividends declared after reporting date: disclose, don't recognise

### MFRS 112 — Income Taxes
- Current tax: amount payable/recoverable for current period
- Deferred tax: temporary differences between carrying amount and tax base
- Recognition: all taxable temporary differences (exceptions: goodwill, initial recognition exemption)
- Measurement: enacted/substantively enacted rates expected to apply when realised/settled
- DTA recognised only to extent probable future taxable profits available

### MFRS 116 — Property, Plant and Equipment
- Recognition: probable future economic benefits + cost reliably measurable
- Initial measurement: cost (purchase price + directly attributable costs + decommissioning estimate)
- Subsequent: cost model OR revaluation model (policy per class)
- Depreciation: systematic over useful life; component approach for significant components
- Derecognition: gain/loss = proceeds - carrying amount → P&L

### MFRS 119 — Employee Benefits
- Short-term: expected to be settled within 12 months (salaries, annual leave, bonuses)
- Post-employment: defined contribution (expense as contributed) or defined benefit (actuarial valuation)
- Malaysian context: EPF is defined contribution plan
- Termination benefits: recognise at earlier of (a) entity can no longer withdraw offer, (b) recognises restructuring costs

### MFRS 121 — Foreign Currency
- Functional currency: currency of primary economic environment
- Presentation currency: may differ from functional
- Foreign transactions: translate at transaction date rate
- Monetary items at reporting date: closing rate
- Exchange differences: P&L (except qualifying cash flow hedges → OCI)

### MFRS 123 — Borrowing Costs
- Capitalise borrowing costs directly attributable to qualifying asset
- Qualifying asset: takes substantial period to get ready for use/sale
- All other borrowing costs: expense as incurred
- No choice under MFRS (must capitalise for qualifying assets)

### MFRS 124 — Related Party Disclosures
- Key management personnel compensation (short-term, post-employment, other long-term, termination, share-based)
- Related party transactions: nature, amount, outstanding balances, terms, guarantees
- Parent-subsidiary relationship: always disclose (even if no transactions)

### MFRS 133 — Earnings Per Share
- Basic EPS: profit attributable to ordinary equity holders / weighted average ordinary shares
- Diluted EPS: adjust for potentially dilutive instruments (options, convertibles, contingent shares)
- Required for listed entities

### MFRS 136 — Impairment of Assets
- Test when indicators exist (annually for goodwill and indefinite-life intangibles)
- Recoverable amount = higher of fair value less costs of disposal AND value in use
- Value in use: PV of expected future cash flows
- Impairment loss: carrying amount - recoverable amount → P&L (or revaluation reserve if revalued)
- Cash-generating units (CGU): smallest group generating independent cash inflows

### MFRS 137 — Provisions, Contingent Liabilities and Contingent Assets
- Provision: present obligation + probable outflow + reliable estimate
- Contingent liability: disclose (possible obligation, or probable but not reliably estimable)
- Contingent asset: disclose if probable (never recognise until virtually certain)
- Best estimate: expected value or most likely outcome (single obligation)

### MFRS 138 — Intangible Assets
- Recognition: identifiable, control, future economic benefits, cost reliably measurable
- Internally generated: research → expense; development → capitalise if 6 criteria met
- Subsequent: cost model or revaluation model (active market required for revaluation)
- Amortise over useful life (indefinite life: no amortisation, annual impairment test)

### MFRS 140 — Investment Property
- Property held to earn rentals or for capital appreciation (not owner-occupied, not inventory)
- Initial: cost
- Subsequent: fair value model (changes → P&L) OR cost model (policy choice for all)
- Transfer: when change in use (evidence-based)

## MFRS vs MPERS: Key Differences

| Area | MFRS | MPERS |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | 5-step model (MFRS 15) | S23: exchange of goods/services |
| Leases | All on balance sheet (MFRS 16) | Finance/operating distinction (S20) |
| Financial Instruments | Classification + ECL (MFRS 9) | Simpler: basic + other (S11/S12) |
| Consolidation | Required (MFRS 10) | Required (S9) but less complex |
| Investment Property | Fair value option (MFRS 140) | Cost-depreciation-impairment only (S16) |
| Borrowing Costs | Must capitalise (MFRS 123) | Choice: capitalise or expense (S25) |
| EPS | Required (MFRS 133) | Not required |
| Segment Reporting | Required (MFRS 8) | Not required |
| Cash Flow Statement | Required (MFRS 107) | Required (S7) |
| Intangibles | Development costs capitalised (MFRS 138) | All intangibles: finite life assumed (S18) |

## Disclosure Checklist (Minimum for Berhad)

1. Basis of preparation and statement of compliance with MFRS
2. Significant accounting policies (for each material class of transactions/balances)
3. Revenue disaggregation (by type, geography, timing)
4. Segment reporting (reportable segments per MFRS 8)
5. EPS (basic and diluted)
6. Related party disclosures
7. Financial instruments (classification, fair value hierarchy, risk management)
8. PPE (reconciliation of carrying amounts)
9. Intangible assets (reconciliation)
10. Lease disclosures (maturity analysis, ROU asset reconciliation)
11. Income tax (reconciliation of effective rate, DTA/DTL movements)
12. Contingent liabilities and commitments
13. Events after reporting period
14. Key management compensation
15. Dividend information
