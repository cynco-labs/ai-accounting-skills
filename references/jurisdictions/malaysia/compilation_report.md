# Compilation Engagement — ISRS 4410 (Revised)

## Scope

A compilation engagement is performed when:
- Client needs financial statements prepared from their records
- No assurance (audit/review) is being expressed
- Accountant applies accounting expertise to assist management

This is the most common engagement type for small/medium clients at this firm.

---

## Accountant's Responsibilities

1. Obtain understanding of the entity (business, accounting system, framework)
2. Compile financial information into financial statements
3. Communicate with management as needed
4. Ensure compiled FS are not misleading (professional judgement)
5. Report on compilation (accountant's report attached to FS)

## Management's Responsibilities

1. Accuracy and completeness of records provided
2. Internal controls and fraud prevention
3. Compliance with laws and regulations
4. Selecting applicable financial reporting framework
5. All information provided to accountant is complete and accurate

---

## Compilation Report Template

```
ACCOUNTANT'S COMPILATION REPORT

To the Director(s) / Owner(s) of [Entity Name]

We have compiled the accompanying financial statements of [Entity Name]
([Registration No.]) based on information provided by management. These
financial statements comprise the Statement of Financial Position as at
[date], the Statement of Comprehensive Income, Statement of Changes in
Equity [and Statement of Cash Flows] for the financial year then ended,
and notes to the financial statements including a summary of significant
accounting policies.

We performed this compilation engagement in accordance with International
Standard on Related Services 4410 (Revised), Compilation Engagements.

We have applied our expertise in accounting and financial reporting to
assist management in the preparation and presentation of these financial
statements in accordance with [Malaysian Private Entities Reporting
Standards (MPERS) / Malaysian Financial Reporting Standards (MFRS)]. We
have complied with relevant ethical requirements, including principles of
integrity, objectivity, professional competence, and due care.

These financial statements and the accuracy and completeness of the
information used to compile them are management's responsibility.

Since a compilation engagement is not an assurance engagement, we are not
required to verify the accuracy or completeness of the information
management provided to us for the compilation of these financial statements.
Accordingly, we do not express an audit opinion or a review conclusion on
these financial statements.

[Firm Name]
[Registration Number]
[Address]

[Date]
```

---

## Variations

### Sole Proprietor / Partnership
Replace "Director(s)" with "Owner" or "Partners". Replace company-specific terms as appropriate.

### With Emphasis of Matter
If there are material uncertainties (e.g., going concern) that do not affect the compilation itself but should be highlighted:

```
Without modifying our conclusion above, we draw attention to Note [X]
to the financial statements which describes [the material uncertainty].
```

### When Framework Not Fully Complied With
If departures from framework exist (and management agrees to disclose):

```
We draw attention to Note [X] to the financial statements, which describes
that [Entity Name] has departed from [specific MPERS/MFRS requirement]
because [reason]. As a result, [impact on financial statements].
```

---

## Engagement Letter (Key Clauses)

Before starting a compilation engagement, confirm in writing:
1. Objective and scope: prepare FS from records provided
2. Management's responsibility for records, completeness, accuracy
3. Accountant's responsibility to compile per ISRS 4410
4. Framework to be applied (MPERS/MFRS/other)
5. Expected form and content of accountant's report
6. Limitation: no assurance provided
7. Fees and billing terms

---

## Financial Statement Presentation (Compilation)

The compiled financial statements follow the same presentation standards as any other financial statements under the applicable framework (MPERS or MFRS). The only addition is:
- The accountant's compilation report is attached as the first page after the cover
- Every page of the FS bears the note: "Refer to Accountant's Compilation Report"

The financial statements themselves are prepared with the same rigour as if they were being audited — the difference is only in the level of assurance expressed, not in the quality of the underlying accounting work.

---

## Documentation Requirements

The accountant must maintain:
1. Engagement letter (signed)
2. Working papers (source data, calculations, classifications)
3. Communication with management (queries raised, responses received)
4. Copy of compiled financial statements and compilation report
5. Record of professional judgements applied

Retention: minimum 7 years (to match ITA record-keeping requirement).
