# MPERS Framework Reference (Malaysian Private Entities Reporting Standards)

Applies to: **Sdn Bhd (private limited companies)** and **PLT (limited liability partnerships)** that are not required to apply MFRS.

MPERS is issued by MASB, based on IFRS for SMEs with Malaysian modifications.

---

## Detailed Application by Section

### S2 — Concepts and Pervasive Principles

**Qualitative characteristics:** relevance, reliability (faithful representation, substance over form, prudence, completeness), comparability, timeliness, balance of benefit vs cost.

**Recognition criteria:**
- Asset: probable future economic benefit, reliably measurable
- Liability: probable outflow, reliably measurable
- Income: increase in asset or decrease in liability (beyond equity contributions)
- Expense: decrease in asset or increase in liability (beyond equity distributions)

**Measurement bases:**
- Historical cost (default for most items)
- Fair value (financial instruments under S12, investment property if policy choice)
- Present value (provisions where time value material)

### S4 — Statement of Financial Position

**Current assets** (any one criterion):
- Expected to be realised in normal operating cycle
- Held primarily for trading
- Expected to be realised within 12 months
- Cash/cash equivalent (unless restricted > 12 months)

**Current liabilities** (any one criterion):
- Expected to be settled in normal operating cycle
- Held primarily for trading
- Due within 12 months
- No unconditional right to defer > 12 months

### S5 — Statement of Comprehensive Income

Two approaches allowed:
1. **Single statement**: P&L + OCI in one statement
2. **Two statements**: separate Income Statement + Statement of Comprehensive Income

OCI items under MPERS (limited):
- Foreign currency translation differences (S30)
- Gains/losses on hedging instruments (S12)
- Actuarial gains/losses on defined benefit plans (if applicable)

**Minimum line items:**
- Revenue
- Finance costs
- Share of profit/loss of associates/JVs (equity method)
- Tax expense
- Profit or loss
- Each component of OCI
- Total comprehensive income

### S7 — Statement of Cash Flows

**Classification:**
- Operating: principal revenue-producing activities
- Investing: acquisition/disposal of long-term assets
- Financing: changes in equity and borrowings

**Method:** indirect method is most common (start from profit, adjust for non-cash items and working capital changes).

**Key adjustments (indirect method):**
- Add back: depreciation, amortisation, impairment, loss on disposal
- Remove: gain on disposal, interest income (if classified as investing)
- Working capital: (increase)/decrease in receivables, (increase)/decrease in inventory, increase/(decrease) in payables

### S11 — Basic Financial Instruments

**Basic instruments** (measured at amortised cost):
- Cash
- Demand and fixed-term deposits
- Trade receivables/payables
- Notes receivable/payable
- Loans (fixed or variable market rate, no conversion features)
- Commitments to receive/make loans that cannot be net settled

**Initial measurement:** transaction price (usually = fair value) unless arrangement constitutes financing (then PV)

**Subsequent measurement:**
- Debt instruments: amortised cost using effective interest method
- Equity investments in subsidiary/associate: cost less impairment
- Other equity investments: fair value through P&L (if FV readily determinable), else cost less impairment

**Impairment:**
- Assess at each reporting date: is there objective evidence?
- Individual or group assessment
- Reversal: only to extent carrying amount doesn't exceed what amortised cost would have been
- Write-off: when no reasonable expectation of recovery

### S13 — Inventories

- **Cost:** purchase + conversion + other costs to bring to location/condition
- **Cost formulas:** FIFO or weighted average (entity must use same formula for similar items)
- **Measurement:** lower of cost and estimated selling price less costs to complete and sell
- **Recognition of expense:** when sold (COGS), or written down
- **Disclosure:** policies, carrying amount, amount recognised as expense, write-downs, reversals

### S17 — Property, Plant and Equipment

**Recognition:** probable future economic benefits + cost reliably measurable

**Initial measurement:** cost = purchase price + directly attributable costs + estimated decommissioning

**Component approach:** if components have significantly different patterns of consumption, depreciate separately (e.g., roof vs structure)

**Subsequent measurement:** cost model ONLY (no revaluation model under MPERS)

**Depreciation:**
- Begin when available for use
- Cease when derecognised (not when idle, unless units-of-production)
- Method: straight-line, diminishing balance, or units of production — reflects pattern of benefit consumption
- Review residual value, useful life, and method at least each reporting date
- Change = change in estimate (prospective)

**Derecognition:** gain/loss = proceeds less carrying amount → P&L

### S20 — Leases

**Classification** (based on substance, risks and rewards):

**Finance lease indicators:**
- Transfer of ownership by end of lease
- Bargain purchase option
- Lease term = major part of economic life
- PV of minimum lease payments ≈ substantially all of fair value
- Specialised asset only lessee can use without major modifications

**Lessee — Finance lease:**
- Recognise asset + liability at lower of FV and PV of minimum lease payments
- Depreciate: shorter of lease term and useful life (unless reasonably certain of ownership transfer)
- Allocate payments: finance charge (effective interest) + principal reduction

**Lessee — Operating lease:**
- Expense: straight-line over lease term (even if payments not on straight-line basis)
- No asset/liability on balance sheet

### S23 — Revenue

**Revenue recognition criteria:**

**Sale of goods:**
1. Significant risks and rewards of ownership transferred
2. No continuing managerial involvement or effective control
3. Amount reliably measurable
4. Probable economic benefits will flow
5. Costs incurred/to be incurred reliably measurable

**Rendering of services:**
- If outcome estimable: percentage of completion
- If not estimable: recognise revenue only to extent of recoverable expenses (zero-profit method)

**Construction contracts:**
- Same as rendering of services (percentage of completion if estimable)

**Interest:** effective interest method (time-proportion)
**Royalties:** accrual basis per substance of agreement
**Dividends:** when right to receive established

### S25 — Borrowing Costs

**Policy choice** (entity-wide, applied consistently):
- Option 1: Expense all borrowing costs as incurred
- Option 2: Capitalise borrowing costs directly attributable to qualifying assets

Most Sdn Bhd companies choose to **expense** for simplicity.

### S27 — Impairment of Assets

**When to test:** at each reporting date, assess whether any indicator of impairment exists.

**Indicators (external):**
- Decline in market value
- Significant adverse changes in environment (technological, market, economic, legal)
- Increase in market interest rates
- Carrying amount of net assets > market capitalisation

**Indicators (internal):**
- Obsolescence or physical damage
- Significant adverse changes in extent/manner of use
- Asset's performance worse than expected

**Measurement:**
- Recoverable amount = higher of (a) fair value less costs to sell, (b) value in use
- Value in use = PV of expected future cash flows (pre-tax discount rate)
- If individual asset's recoverable amount cannot be determined → test at CGU level

**Reversal:** allowed (except goodwill). Reverse if indicators have reversed. Not above carrying amount that would have existed without impairment.

### S29 — Income Tax

**Current tax:**
- Amount payable (or recoverable) for current/prior periods
- Measured at enacted/substantively enacted rates

**Deferred tax:**
- Temporary difference: carrying amount (MPERS) ≠ tax base (ITA 1967)
- Taxable temporary difference → DTL (future tax payable)
- Deductible temporary difference → DTA (future tax benefit)
- DTA: recognise only if probable that future taxable profit available

**Common temporary differences in Malaysia:**
| Item | Carrying Amount vs Tax Base |
|---|---|
| PPE (depreciation vs CA) | Usually taxable TD |
| Provisions (not yet deductible) | Deductible TD |
| Unabsorbed losses | Deductible TD (DTA) |
| Revaluation gains (if any) | Taxable TD |

**Presentation:**
- Current tax: liability (or asset if overpaid)
- Deferred tax: non-current (no discounting)

### S30 — Foreign Currency Translation

**Functional currency:** determined by primary economic environment (where entity generates/spends cash)

**Transactions in foreign currency:**
- Initial: translate at spot rate on transaction date (or average for period if rates don't fluctuate significantly)
- Monetary items at reporting date: closing rate
- Non-monetary items at historical cost: transaction date rate
- Non-monetary items at FV: rate when FV determined

**Exchange differences:** recognised in P&L in period they arise (except hedging reserves)

### S33 — Related Party Disclosures

**Related parties include:**
- Parent, subsidiaries, fellow subsidiaries
- Associates, joint ventures
- Key management personnel (and close family)
- Entities controlled/jointly controlled by key management or their family
- Post-employment benefit plans

**Disclosures required:**
- Parent-subsidiary relationship (even if no transactions)
- Key management compensation (aggregate)
- For each related party transaction: nature of relationship, description of transaction, amount, outstanding balances, terms, provisions for doubtful debts, expense recognised for bad/doubtful debts

## MPERS Disclosure Checklist (Minimum for Sdn Bhd)

1. Statement of compliance with MPERS
2. Basis of preparation (going concern, accrual basis)
3. Significant accounting policies (each material policy)
4. Revenue recognition policy
5. PPE: useful lives, depreciation methods, reconciliation of carrying amounts
6. Trade receivables: aging analysis, impairment provision
7. Related party transactions (S33)
8. Directors' remuneration (CA 2016 requirement)
9. Income tax: current year charge, reconciliation of effective rate, DTA/DTL
10. Contingent liabilities (if any)
11. Events after reporting period (if any)
12. Comparative information (prior year for all amounts)

## Small Entity Exemption (MPERS S1)

A private entity qualifies for S1 (simplified) if it meets the thresholds set by MASB (currently):
- Revenue ≤ RM3 million
- Total assets ≤ RM3 million
- Employees ≤ 5 (average during FY)

If qualifying, reduced disclosure requirements apply. However, recognition and measurement remain the same.

## Depreciation Rates (Accounting — Straight-Line Default)

| Asset Category | Useful Life | Annual Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Building | 50 years | 2% |
| Renovation & Improvements | 10 years (or lease term if shorter) | 10% |
| Office Equipment & Furniture | 10 years | 10% |
| Computer Equipment & Software | 3 years | 33.33% |
| Photography/Video Equipment | 5 years | 20% |
| Motor Vehicle | 5 years | 20% |
| Plant & Machinery | 10 years | 10% |
| Signage & Fixtures | 10 years | 10% |

Pro-rate from acquisition date. If acquired mid-year, depreciate proportionally.

## Common Judgement Areas

1. **Lease classification**: Is it finance or operating? Apply indicators test rigorously.
2. **Revenue timing**: Has control/risk transferred? For services, is percentage of completion appropriate?
3. **Impairment indicators**: Don't ignore indicators; don't impair without evidence.
4. **Related parties**: Cast the net wide — check directors' other interests.
5. **Going concern**: If doubt exists, disclose. If material uncertainty, consider whether MPERS basis appropriate.
6. **Deferred tax**: Only recognise DTA if probable future profits — challenge for loss-making companies.
