# Design Context

### Users
Owners doing their own books, bookkeepers, and firm staff — **one product**, three operators. Same six jobs; tone and depth adapt. They arrive stressed (behind on books), curious (evaluating), or mid-job (need a clear next step). Technical level varies: never assume MPERS/TB literacy in first contact; offer precision when `operator: firm` or they ask.

### Brand Personality
**Warm · helpful · confident.** Like a careful colleague who already read the folder — not a sales bot, not a cold audit memo. Empathetic on blockers; brief on success; never blames the user.

### Aesthetic Direction
Site follows the stats-shell / utilitarian open-source lander (not decorative marketing). Copy should feel **plain English first** (CONTEXT.md), short status boards, specific CTAs. Anti-references: firm-only jargon walls, “engagement pipeline” theatre for owners, dual install menus as the hero.

### Design Principles
1. **One front door** — “Do the accounting” beats a slash menu.
2. **Say what happens next** — every status, error, and empty state has a next action.
3. **Depth-honest** — books-only never sounds like unfinished year-end failure.
4. **Same words everywhere** — shelf, books, trial balance, prove (see CONTEXT.md).
5. **Warm without fluff** — cut jargon; keep numbers and gates serious.
